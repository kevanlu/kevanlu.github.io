      Functional Gut Disorders

      Jeremy Chitpin Thursday, September 11, 2014

      ###Common Functional GI Disorder Symptoms###

      • Globus sensation
        • “lump in the throat that can’t be cleared”
      • Atypical chest pain - noncardiac
      • Functional dyspepsia
      • Functional Bowel Disorders
      • Proctalgia fugax
        • severe, episode, recurring anal pain

      ###Define Functional Disorders###

      • Abnormality in function
      • yet no abnormalities in anatomy, biochemistry, or upon pathology
      • many of these diseases may be psychosomatic (debated)
      • often no “cure,” therefore bedside manner is crucial

      Irritable Bowel Syndrome

      • Most common GI disorder
      • The #2 cause of work absenteeism
      • Given that IBS is so common, most ptx never see an MD
      • For a patient to be seeing an MD, must be fairly serious, or patient must be fairly determined

      IBS Diagnosis

      • Use the Rome III Criteria:

      ####Rome III Criteria

      • Recurrent abdominal pain or discomfort, 􏰋>3 days/month in the last 3 months, associated with ≥ 2 of:
        1. Improvement with defecation.
        2. Onset associated with change in frequency of stool.
        3. Onset associated with change in form (appearance) of stool.

      Supportive symptoms:

      • too often or too few stools
      • straining
      • incomplete emptying

      ###IBS: Pathophysiology###

      • poorly understood
      • Post infectious IBS, after traumatic infection
      • Altered GI motility
      • Visceral hyperalgesia
      • Food sensitivity
      • Psychopathology

      ###1. Altered GI Motility###

      • Gut overreacts to stretch
      • exaggeration to food in bowel
      • hypercontractility in the bowel
      • uncoordinated contractions, or prolonged

      ###2. Visceral Hyperalgesia###

      • gut is oversensitive, interprets stretch or normal motility as pain
      • could be fear of pain from eating
      • could be greater GI motor response to stress

      ###3. Intestinal Microflora / Infection###

      • After infectious enteritis, microflora is disturbed
      • antibiotic use can also cause IBS
      • persistent symptoms after infectious diarrhea

      ###4. Psychopathology###

      • anxious people may experience pain more strongly
      • pain may cause additional anxiety

      ###5. Food Sensitivity ###

      • food sensitivity (not allergy) may exacerabate IBS

      ###Quality of Life for IBS Patients##

      • QOL is lower than the norm
      • Worse than even diabetes or End Stage Renal Disease
      • IBS have higher incidences of depression, fatigue, insomnia

      ###Work up for IBS###

      • careful history
      • rule out other conditions and differential
      • look for co-associated conditions
        • fibromyalgia, headache, backache, GU symptoms, emotional distress, depression

      Red Flags: Nocturnal or progressive pain Family history of colon cancer or inflammatory bowel disease or weight loss Anemia or iron deficiency or bleeding Abdominal mass These suggest IBD or malignancy

      Investigations for IBS

      • CBC to screen for anemia, inflammation, infection
      • ESR or CRP for inflammation
      • FOBT
      • Stool Ova & Parasites for parasitic infection
      • Over 40-50 yo, can be referred for colonoscopy
      • if more diarrheal, screen for celiac
        • use Serum tissue transglutaminase (tTG) antibody for celiac
      • serum calcium for hyperparathyroidism
      • check for food insensitivity such as lactose intolerance

      ###Management###

      • Set expectations and supportiv therapeutic relationship
      • Provide reassurance
      • Dietary modification
        • to rule out food insensitivity
      • Physical activity
      • Medications and CAM not that useful

      ###Additional management for IBS### - Elevate feet when defecating - Morning Post-Prandial gastro-colonic response: - To accomodate a morning meal, body will make room for new food by pushing waste down to colon - Fibre - increased H2O intake - Avoid caffeine - Avoid poor food triggers - legumes, or gluten, or lactose

      ###FODMAPs###

      • FODMAPs are fermentable oligodimonosaccharides and polyols
      • sugars not easily digested, enter colon,
      • act as osmoles and bacterial substrates, cause diarrhea
      • avoidance of FODMAPs may help IBS and diarrhea

      ###Psychological support###

      • even in real pathological diseases such as IBD, placebo effect is ~40%!
      • IBS is strongly correlated with negative emotional affect, conditions such as depression

      ###Drugs for constipation###

      • drugs are symptomatic relief, not cures
      • Fibre supplement
        • Metamucil, etc
      • Laxatives
      • Loperamide - immodium
      • antidepressants and antispasmodics can help, or can worsen constipation
      • Probiotics may show benefit

      ###Remember, treat the person, not just the bowel!###

      Functional Dyspepsia

      • Dyspepsia
        • pain or discomfort of the abdomen

      • functional = no evidence of organic disease

      • 25% have underlying organic cause and it is not functional dyspepsia

      ###Screening for Dyspepsia###

      • consider drugs like NSAIDS
      • H pylori
      • neoplasia, pancreatitis
      • heavy drinking

      Symptoms

      • ulcer like pain in epigastrium
      • nausea, vomitting, early satiety
      • gas, belching
      • Pathophysiology similar to IBS
        • Primary Differences between Functional Dyspepsia and IBS:
          • Symptoms not alleviated by bowel movements**
          • Symptoms relate to eating as opposed to bowel movement

      ###Workup###

      • CBC
      • serology for H pylori
      • Endoscopy if red flags
      • 24h pH study if atypical GERD suspected
        • this checks for pathologic reflux, if pain coincides with acid reflux

      Red Flags similar to IBS, see slide

      Treatment

      • if H pylori positive treat H pylori with PPI and Abx (see previous lectures)
      • if H pylori negative reassurance, validation
        • diet modification
        • alcohol and smoking reduction
        • exercise
        • discontinue aggravating drugs
      • Consider prescribing prokinetics
        • Domperidone (Note, arrythmia black box warning)
      • or Antidepressants (TCAs)
