                            Embryology – Development of the GI System

                            Kevan Lu Tuesday, September 02, 2014
                            • Find notes online!
                            • Super important.
                            • Slides are more of a talking point thing.

                            Take Home Points

                            • Tube suspended by a membrane called the mesentery.
                            • 3 parts → foregut, midgut, hindgut.
                            • Each part has a separate blood supply coming out of the aorta. These travel in the mesentary.
                            • Rotation of the foregut and midgut.
                            • The hindgut opens into the cloaca with the allantois, and will be separated by the urorectal septum (remember this from Embryology of the Kidney).
                            • Lines of fusion of the mesentery create intraperitoneal and retroperitoneal structures.

                            The Foregut, Midgut, and Hindgut

                            • Below is a sagittal view of the developing embryo:

                            • The developing GI tract is a tube suspended by a mesentery (in grey).
                            • This tube has 3 parts:
                              • Foregut supplied by the coeliac artery.
                              • Midgut supplied by the superior mesenteric artery.
                              • Hindgut supplied by the inferior mesenteric artery.

                            • This is useful, because if you know the developmental origin of a specific region, you’ll know it’s blood supply!

                            • LISTEN TO THIS LATER.
                            • The foregut has a ventral and a dorsal mesentery.
                            • The mesenteries help suspend the tube in a big open space.
                            • Developing out of the foregut (in vellow), are some diverticuli, which become the pancreas, gallbladder, and the liver.
                            • Hence, the blood supply of the liver is the coeliac artery!
                            • The hindgut opens into the cloaca, which is a common exit point with the allantois (the urinary system).

                            - Omentum = draping of the mesentery. - LISTEN TO THIS LATER!

                            The Foregut

                            Rotation of the Foregut

                            • Both the foregut and the midgut undergo rotation.
                            • Rotation of the foregut especially involves the stomach.
                              • The stomach rotates longitudinally.
                              • The dorsal side curves more rapidly than the ventral side, forming the classic stomach shape.
                              • Then it rotates horizontally, to end up in its final position in the body.
                            • The mesentery that wraps around the stomach then creates a space behind the stomach.
                            • That is the lesser sac.
                            • Everything out there is the greater sac.
                            • Can go from greater sac to lesser sac from a gap at the end of the ventral mesentery.

                            • Innervated by the vagus.

                            ROTATION OF THE DIVERTICULI - Original location, liver, gallpladder, pancreas bud juts out in front (ventrally). - Once it rotates, they swing around to the right (more right oriented), and the stomac ends up more on the left. - But the pancreatic buds swinger further. - What’s then 1 pancreas comes from 2 buds. - LISTEN TO THIS LATER. - Superior mesenteric artery gets sandwiched between the 2 buds of the pancreas.
