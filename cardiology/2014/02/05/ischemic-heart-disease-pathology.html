    Ischemic Heart Disease Pathology

    Kevan Lu Wednesday, February 05, 2014

    Lecture Objectives

    • Define ischemic heart disease (IHD).
    • Correlate features of coronary artery anatomy relevant to IHD.
    • Explain the underlying pathological similarity between the four manifestations of IHD: angina, myocardial infarction, chronic ischemic heart disease, and sudden cardiac death.
    • Describe the critical role coronary thrombosis plays in the pathogenesis of myocardial infarction.
    • Explain the pathological differences between subendocardial and transmural myocardial infarcts.
    • Describe the pathological process of myocardial infarct healing.
    • Explain the cause and mechanism of death in most cases of sudden cardiac death.

    Cardiac Vascular Anatomy

    • The heart is highly vascular due to its high metabolic demand.
    • You need to know the coronary artery anatomy, because it’s critical in interpreting ECGs, and managing patients with MIs.
    • When you deal with an anterior MI, you’re looking at the anterior wall of the left ventricle, you’re not looking at the anterior part of the heart (which anatomically, is the right ventricle).
    • Since the LV does so much more work than the RV, it’s the one that’s typically more susceptible to ischemia.

    • With respect to the Left Ventricle, the different parts are perfused by:

      • Anterior Wall → perfused by the LAD (left anterior descending).
      • Posterior Wall → perfused by the RCA (right coronary artery).
      • Lateral Wall → perfused by the left circumflex (off the left coronary artery).
      • Septal Wall → 2/3 by the LAD, 1/3 by the RCA. This varies depending on whether you’re left dominant or right dominant however.
