                                  Case of the Week – Knee Pain

                                  Kevan Lu Monday, February 02, 2015

                                  Knee Pain Case

                                  Case Summary

                                  38 year old man says he was minding his own business at work today carrying a 100 lb load up a ramp when he fell. He reports the ramp was wet due to a leaky rood and that other coworkers have had twisted ankles as a result. He thinks he landed on the anterior knee and may have twisted to drop the box he was carrying. He reports the pain was severe and unrelenting. He is able to bend and straighten the knee but not all the way because it hurts. He has not attempted to walk on it yet. Now he feels lightheaded and has not had anything to drink since his XL coffee this morning (6 h ago).

                                  Relevant info: 39 y/o man. at work. 100 lb load. fell. landed on anterior knee. Able to bend and straighten the knee but not all the way b/c it hurts.

                                  Acute Traumatic Knee Pain DDx: Select up to 5

                                  ACL Tear Patellofemoral syndrome. Patella fracture Patella dislocation Osgoode-Schlatter’s MCL tear Meniscus tear Per anserine bursitis Iliotibial band snydrome Septic knee joint Compartment syndrome Bleeding Disorder

                                  Acute, TRAUMA. ACL tear Patella # Patella dislocation MCL tear Meniscus tear

                                  Had an acute patella dislocation. Immediately reduced in 1986. Had some (?) surgical intervention at the time. - Since then, has had persistent L knee pain since trauma x 10 years. - Various courses of analgesics and therapy. - Multiple scopes, lavages, and antibiotics. - Never able to return to work at refinery. - Standing/walking/lifting intolerance. - Became an insurance salesman. - X-Ray

                                  2 views. AP view ‘Skyline’ view. Loss of joint space. Patella Joint space narrowed, osteophytes. Basically bone on bone (femur against tibia). Seems like bone is compacted, subchondral sclerosus. Advanced OA of knee at age of 50.

                                  • How do we treat OA?
                                    • Weight-loss, exercise (unload the joint).
                                    • Steroid injections.
                                    • Medications (NSAIDs, Tylenol, anti-depressants).
                                    • Cane, offloader brace.
                                  • TKA was recommended: Total Knee Arthroplasty.
                                    • All conservative measures were exhausted.
                                    • Pt asks:
                                      • Can you guarantee that this will help my pain?
                                      • What is my post-op course?
                                      • What’s the plan to get back to work/activity.
