            End of course Exam Format

            • Less than 50 MC questions.
            • 21-ish extended match questions.
            • 7 or less short answers.
            • 1 XR.
            • No LMCC key features.
            • No essays.
            • No funny stuff.

            Basic Competency Examination

            1. What common problem must all newborns be examined for?
              • Hip dysplasia, developmental dysplasia of the hip.
            2. What is a compartment syndrome.
              • Increased pressure in a fascial compartment, leading to injury.
            3. Acute septic arthritis can be differentiated from inflammatory arthritis by what?
              • Joint aspiration:
                • Cell Count.
                • Culture & Sensitivity.
                • Crystals.
                • Gram Stain.
            4. A patient dislocated his knee in a car accident. What structure(s) is/are at risk for injury and therefore must be evaluated?
              • Popliteal artery.
            5. A patient punches his companion in the face and sustains a fracture of the 5th metacarpal and a 3-mm break in the skin over the fracture. What is the correct treatment, and why?
              • Boxer’s fracture
              • In addition to typical treatment, we must add:
                • Antibiotics.
                • Irrigation and debridement.
                • Tetanus prophylaxis.
            6. A patient comes to the office complaining of LBP that wakes him up from sleep. What two diagnoses are you concerned about?
              • Pain that wakes someone up from sleep is a RED FLAG.
              • Infection and tumour are 2 things you would worry about.
            7. How is compartment syndrome treated.
              • Fasciotomy (urgent).
            8. A patient lands on his hand and is tender to palpation in the “snuff box” (the space between the thumb extensor and the abductor tendons). Initial radiographs do not show a fracture. What diagnosis must be considered?
              • Scaphoid fracture.
            9. A 25-year old male is involved in a motor vehicle accident. His left limb is in a position of flexion at the knee and hip with internal rotation and adduction at the hip. What is the most likely diagnosis?
              • Hip dislocation.
            10. What nerve is compressed in carpal tunnel syndrome?
              • Median nerve.
            11. What is the classic presentation of carpal tunnel syndrome?
              • Tingling and paresthesia over the palmar first thumb, index, middle fingers.
            12. A patient has a disc herniation pressing on the 5th lumbar nerve root. How is motor function of the 5th lumbar nerve root tested?
              • Walking on your heels.
              • OR function of Extensor Hallicus Longus (dorsiflexion).
              • What if he was a below-knee amputee?
                • Can assess hip abduction (superficial gluteal nerve, which has nerve roots L4, L5, S1).
            13. How is motor function of the median nerve tested in the hand?
              • Action of the LOAF muscles:
                • Lumbricals 1 & 2.
                • Opponens Pollicis.
                • Abductor Pollicis Brevis.
                • Flexor Pollicis Brevis.
            14. A 12-year old boy severely twists his ankle. Radiographs show only soft-tissue swelling. He is tender at the distal aspect of the fibula. What are the 2 possible diagnoses?
              • Would get an X-Ray, positive tenderness 5 cm over lateral malleolus.
              • 2 possible diagnoses.
                • Ligament sprain.
                • Salter-Harris fracture.
            15. A patient presents with new-onset low-back-pain. Under what conditions are plain radiographs indicated? Please name 5:
              1. History of trauma.
              2. Neurologic signs.
              3. Systemic symptoms (weight loss, fatigue, etc).
              4. Age (>50, <20)
              5. Bowel of Bladder changes.
              6. History of Cancer.
              7. Drug & Steroid use.
            16. A patient has a displaced fracture near the fibular neck. What structure is at risk for injury?
              • Common peroneal nerve.
            17. A 20-year old injured his knee while playing football. You see him on the same day, and he has a knee effusion. An aspiration shows frank blood. What are the 3 most common diagnoses?
              1. Ligament tear.
              2. Fracture.
              3. Peripheral meniscal tear (peripheral only because of blood supply).
              4. Capsular tear.
              5. Patellar dislocation.
            18. What are the 5 most common sources of cancer metastatic to bone?
              1. Lung.
              2. Prostate.
              3. Lung.
              4. Kidney.
              5. Thyroid.
            19. Name two differences between rheumatoid arthritis and osteoarthritis.
              1. RA spares the DIP joints, OA spares the MCPs.
              2. RA is inflammatory, OA is degenerative.
            20. What malignancy may be present in bone, yet is typically not detected with a bone scan?
              • Myeloma → any of the hematologic malignancies (leukemia, lymphoma).
            21. What is the function of the normal anterior cruciate ligament at the knee?
              • Prevents the tibia from moving anteriorly with respect to the femur.
            22. What is the difference between osteoporosis and osteomalacia?
              • Osteoporosis → decreased bone density.
              • Osteomalacia → decreased bone mineralization.
            23. In elderly patients, displaced fractures of the femoral neck are typically treated with joint replacement, whereas fractures near the trochanter are treated with plates and screws. Why?
              • Risk of AVN or nonunion of femoral neck, due to poor blood supply.
            24. What muscle(s) is/are involved in lateral epicondylitis (tennis elbow)?
              • The wrist extensors.
                • Extensor carpi radialis brevis.
                • Extensor carpi radialis longus.
                • Extensor digitorum communis.
            25. Rupture of the biceps at the elbow results in weakness of both elbow flexion and?
              • Supination.
            26. What muscle(s) control(s) external rotation of the humerus with the arm at the side?
              1. Infraspinatus.
              2. Teres minor.
