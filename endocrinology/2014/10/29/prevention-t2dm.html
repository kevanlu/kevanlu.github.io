          Prevention of T2DM

          Kevan Lu Wednesday, October 29, 2014

          T2DM Prevention Notes

          • See T2DM Prevention Notes.

          Lecture Objectives

          1. Identify types of prevention as they apply to diabetes.
          2. Describe effective methods of preventing Type 2 diabetes.
          3. Differentiate issues of diabetes prevention as they apply to individuals and populations.

          Primary Prevention

          • Recall that there are 3 types of prevention in medicine:
            1. Primary → the disease never happens.
            2. Secondary → the disease is stopped or prevented from becoming worse.
            3. Tertiary → the chronic complications of disease are prevented from getting worse.
          • Today, we’ll be focusing on primary prevention.

          Primary Prevention of Type 1 Diabetes

          TYPE 1 DIABETS. What has been tried and failed • Nicotinamide • Immunization with low dose insulin s/c • Oral insulin • Cow’s milk study in infants still going on (called Trigr) - Homology for ab for cows milk and ab for islets cells. - So if you get cows milk too soon, you can set up an Ab response, triggering beta cells. - Trial is still 10 years down. - Preliminary results look ugly. - Study in early 80s with ciclosporine. - In patients in honeymoon period of T1DM, still some islet cells working. - They prolonged the honeymoon! - The problem was that patients got lymphomas from the anti-rejection drugs. - So the negatives outweighed the benefits. SO WE STILL HAVE NOTHING GOOD.

          Primary Prevention of Type 2 Diabetes

          • Wasn’t thought possible • However, last few years have seen great and fascinating studies

          Why do we care? ! Lifetime risk of developing diabetes (type 1 or type 2) for individuals born in 2000 ◦ Male = 33% and Female = 39% ! Average reduction in life years ◦ Male = 12 yrs, and Female = 19 yrs

          Risk factors Impaired glucose tolerance Impared fasting glucose gestsational diabetes (see previous lectuer) Ethnicity (asians, hispanics, blacks, higher risk) Obesity. ! Lifestyle Factors ◦ Exercise ◦ Smoking ! In a meta-analysis of 25 prospective cohort studies, smokers at increased risk of DM2 compared with nonsmokers with pooled RR 1.4

          ! Dietary ◦ western diet high in red meat, processed meat, high fat dairy products, sweets, and desserts

          Type 2 DM occurs due to some combo of 1. Genetic predisposition 2. Lowered insulin secretion 3. Insulin resistance demanding more insulin output

          ! Genetics: can’t do a thing except choose your parents carefully ! Insulin secretion: don’t know if anything can be done ! Insulin resistance: lots can be done

          People are insulin resistant due to - We eat lots of fat. - We have no muscle. - We have lots of visceral fat stores. - Still things we don’t know.

          STUDY- Patients with Impaired GLucose Tolerance. First studies: lifestyle Da Qing in China and the Finnish study: • Exercise • Low fat diet

          ! 577 lean + overweight IGT (1986-1992, China) were randomly assigned to four groups; ! Followed for 6 yrs

          Control 67.7% Diet Only 43.8% Exercise Only41.1% Diet and Exercise 46.0% -> HA! Don’t show them these numbers. !31% Risk Reduction!

          Or: said another way………. ! Lifestyle prevented about 30% of people with IGT from tipping over into diabetes range BGs • Subjects had IGT

          ! Low calorie ! Low fat (<30% energy consumed) ! Low saturated fat (<10% energy consumed) ! High fibre diet (>15g/1000kcal) ! Moderate- intensity physical activity of at least 30 minutes/day ! ~5% of initial body wt loss
