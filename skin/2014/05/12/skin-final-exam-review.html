Snow Day Material

  1. Acne and Rosacea.

    • Acne → Know the pathophysiology.
    • Know the treatment → when to use what.
    • Know the acne lesions → comedomes, etc.
    • Don’t need to know dosages, etc. Just focus on when you’d use each.
    • Rosacea → you should know the difference between it and acne, in how it presents, etc.
    • Know that there are no comedomes. They often present in the same populations, so this is important!
    • Know the 4 subtypes of Rosacea → the most common is the erythematotelangiectatic subtype.

  2. Psoriasis.

    • Dr. Guenther is the world expert on Psoriasis, so she thinks everything is important! So there’s lot of excess information on the slides!
    • The onset is important, all those crazy letters and numbers aren’t.
    • Know the difference between intertriginous (flexural) psoriasis and a yeast infection → how demarcated the borders are.
    • Know that psoriasis is associated with arthritis → ask about joint problems with a Hx.
    • Don’t worry about the CASPAR classification stuff!
    • Know the treatment progression: Topicals → phototherapy → systemics. Don’t worry about the details too much.

  3. Papulosquamous Disorders Other Than Psoriasis.

    • Pityriasis Rosea → Herald Patch, self limiting, etc.
    • Lichen Planus → know the P’s!

  4. Dermatitis, Drug Reactions, Urticaria & Blistering Disorders.

    • Dense, lots of material.
    • Contact Dermatitis can be (1) allergic or (2) irritant.
    • Xerotic dermatitis = itchy old people.
    • Blistering disorders are rare. Immunofluorescence is what you should use to diagnose them.
    • Dermatitis herpetiformis has nothing to do with herpes → the discoverer just thought it did!

  5. Cutaneous Manifestations of Systemic Diseases/Internal Malignancy/Vasculitis.

    • Know the cutaneous features of SLE. Dermatomyositis affects the skin and the muscles. Finally, a skin condition that's aptly named!

    • Know the cutaneous features of Dermatomyositis.

    • Know about Henoch-Schonlein Purpura → recall that this is IgA Nephropathy + a skin rash.

    • Know the cutaneous features of diabetes.

      • Acanthosis nigricans → brown velvety patches in the fold areas.
      • Necrobiosis lipoidica → atrophic patches with enlarging erythematous borders, commonly at the legs.
